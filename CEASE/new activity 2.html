Nicotine is addictive: Nicotine affects your brain's A chemical released by the brain that plays a number of roles including motivating behavior, pleasure, rewards, etc. systems in the same way that heroine and cocaine affect an addict's brain. As a smoker, your body becomes conditioned to functioning on a certain level of nicotine. one or two hours after your last cigarette, the nicotine level in your body drops considerably, causing you to crave another cigarette.
